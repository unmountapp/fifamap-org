FIFA World Cup 2026 Complete Travel and Trip Planning Guide

FIFAMap is your complete travel companion for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, the largest football tournament in history. Hosted across 16 cities in the United States, Canada, and Mexico, the 2026 World Cup features 104 matches from June 11 to July 19, 2026. This guide covers match schedules, stadium guides, hotels, transport, fan zones, and AI trip planning.

16 Host Cities and Stadiums

  • Los Angeles, USA — SoFi Stadium (75,000) — 8 matches including semi-final
  • New York/New Jersey, USA — MetLife Stadium (82,500) — 8 matches including the World Cup Final
  • Dallas, USA — AT&T Stadium (80,000) — 9 matches, most of any venue
  • San Francisco Bay Area, USA — Levi's Stadium (68,500) — 7 matches
  • Miami, USA — Hard Rock Stadium (65,000) — 7 matches
  • Atlanta, USA — Mercedes-Benz Stadium (71,000) — 7 matches
  • Seattle, USA — Lumen Field (68,740) — 7 matches, most walkable venue
  • Houston, USA — NRG Stadium (72,000) — 6 matches
  • Boston, USA — Gillette Stadium (65,878) — 6 matches
  • Philadelphia, USA — Lincoln Financial Field (69,328) — 6 matches
  • Kansas City, USA — Arrowhead Stadium (76,416) — 6 matches
  • Mexico City, Mexico — Estadio Azteca (87,523) — 5 matches including tournament opener
  • Guadalajara, Mexico — Estadio Akron (49,850) — 5 matches
  • Monterrey, Mexico — Estadio BBVA (51,348) — 5 matches
  • Toronto, Canada — BMO Field (45,000) — 6 matches
  • Vancouver, Canada — BC Place (54,500) — 6 matches

Match Tickets and Pricing

Official tickets via FIFA.com/tickets. Group stage from $80 USD, Round of 32 from $150, Quarter-finals from $250, Semi-finals from $350, and the World Cup Final from $500. Resale available on StubHub and Viagogo.

Free FIFA Fan Zones

FIFA Fan Festivals in every host city are free-entry fan parks with live match screenings for 50,000+ fans. Watch every game on giant screens with food, entertainment, and merchandise — no ticket required. Fan zones open 3-4 hours before kick-off.

Hotel and Accommodation Guide

Book via Expedia, Hotels.com or Airbnb. Budget: $60-120 per night. Mid-range: $140-280 per night. Luxury: $350 and up. Hotels near stadiums sell out months early. Stay near metro lines to save 30-50 percent on accommodation costs.

Getting to the Stadiums

New York MetLife: NJ Transit from Penn Station. Atlanta: MARTA Blue/Green Line to Vine City. Seattle: Link Light Rail. Mexico City Azteca: Metro Line 2 and Tren Ligero. Dallas AT&T Stadium: official shuttle buses (no direct rail).
